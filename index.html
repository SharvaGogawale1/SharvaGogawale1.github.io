I am a researcher passionate about building AI systems that are socially responsible and impactful. My research journey has been intentionally interdisciplinary, focusing on developing novel AI applications for human-centric fields like healthcare, education, and the digital humanities. My work centers on two main goals: (1) Leveraging the rapid growth of large-scale models to make meaningful contributions to these fields, and (2) Enhancing data-driven methods to build more representative, fair, and unbiased systems. I am actively seeking a PhD position to further this research, with a long-term goal of leading a lab dedicated to AI for social good.

Research

My work focuses on applying AI to human-centric domains. Below are my core research areas.

AI in Healthcare

Developing equitable, non-invasive diagnostic tools. My work includes using diffusion models for 3D reconstruction of coronary arteries from X-rays and SAM-based segmentation for stenosis assessment.

AI in Digital Humanities

Preserving cultural heritage with computer vision. I have developed systems for layout classification, handwriting recognition, and computational paleography for medieval Hebrew manuscripts and Dead Sea Scrolls.

AI in Education & Society

Building systems to improve human experience. This includes real-time attentiveness tracking for e-learning and developing LLM-based agents to detect and mitigate online misinformation.
